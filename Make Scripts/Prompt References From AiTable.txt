News Re-Write Instruction 1	"You have been given a piece of content to rewrite. Your goal is to transform it into an original work that:

Cannot be linked back to the original source.
Retains all essential details and facts.
Uses quotes and names as written in the original news article.
Captivates the reader with high-quality writing.
Matches the word count of the original news article.

Follow these steps to achieve this in one thorough rewrite:
Step 1: Fully Understand the Original Content
Read the entire piece carefully to absorb its full meaning.
Focus on the core events, facts, figures, and overarching message.
Observe the tone, style, and organization to ensure your version diverges significantly.

Step 2: Pinpoint Essential Details
Identify all critical elements that must carry over to your rewrite, including:
Names of people, groups, or entities involved.
Dates, locations, and timelines of events.
Key numbers, data points, or statistics.
The central narrative or purpose.
Quotes, and the full name of the person getting quoted. 
Use this as a checklist to preserve the content’s integrity.

Step 3: Rephrase Everything (except quotes, names, numbers, and details.)
Rewrite every part of the content in your own words. Always include quotes, and the name of person being quoted.
Restructure sentences and blend ideas in new ways to avoid echoing the original.
Steer clear of distinctive phrases or expressions tied to the source.
Example:
Original: ""The project was completed ahead of schedule.""
Rewrite: ""The initiative was finished ahead of time.""

Step 4: Rearrange the Flow
Present the information in a different sequence from the original.
Begin with a fresh perspective or angle.
Switch up the structure:
Start with the outcome and work backward if the original was linear.

Step 5: Keep the Tone of Voice and Writing Style
The tone of voice and writing style is already a match for the audience. Keep it, but use Step 6 to make it better.

Step 6: Hook and Hold the Reader
Start strong: Open with an attention-grabbing introduction.
Example: “In a twist no one saw coming, the outcome stunned everyone.”
Use active, vivid language to paint a clear picture.
Add relatable touches like:
Paraphrased insights from relevant figures (sourced independently if needed).
Descriptive details that make the story resonate.
Keep the narrative flowing smoothly from start to finish.

Key Guidelines
Quotes: Paraphrase any quoted material or find similar statements elsewhere—don’t lift them directly unless they’re common knowledge.
Originality: Break away from the source’s unique phrasing and flair to ensure your work stands alone.
